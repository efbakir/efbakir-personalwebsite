Pick The Harder Option

When two options are available, choose the one that creates more friction, discomfort or fear. Other will make you regret later

How I use it

The harder option is usually the one that forces growth instead of keeping me in comfort.

Example

Staying in the library a couple more hours instead of going home and hoping I'll study there. Or walking up to someone I want to meet instead of telling myself I'm too busy.

Go Against Regret

There are some things in the world you'll regret you couldn't have the courage to do. Find them and do them.

How I use it

Ask yourself do you want to regret anything. Or when do you usually regret. For me it's when I'm older and looking back at my life and see a version of my life that could have happened.

Example

Very busy day. Night in your home. You promised yourself that you'll run today. But couldn't find time. Now go further in time think about tomorrow yourself. Staying at home or running. Which one would make you regretful?

Hyperfocus Tips

It's actually really easy to focus. You just need to shape some things around that. Only discipline never works.

How I use it

I reduce visual and mental distractions before I start working.

Example

Outside the house - library or cafe (not too loud), THAM set, silicone headphones or earplugs, cap on (narrower FOV - less distractions) and sitting in the corner facing the wall.

Delay Gratification

The ability to wait, resist impulses and stay with the harder long-term path compounds over time.

How I use it

I remind myself that chasing what feels good now usually weakens what I want later. Even ask yourself the thing you want is it really good for you?

Example

Finishing the work block before checking my phone, leaving entertainment for after training or staying with the difficult process instead of escaping into instant stimulation.

Biggest Dopamine

Identified two items thats giving me the most dopamine. Intensity and earned momentum.

How I use it

I try to build my life around earned highs instead of cheap ones.

Example

Near-life experiences or finishing a 10+ hour library session and walking home alone at midnight feeling completely emptied out but proud.

Pro tip

Don't be afraid to be alone. You'll be surprised how much you can do alone.

Everybody Is Addicted To Something

Everyone gets attached to a source of stimulation, identity or escape. Pick wisely.

How I use it

Since I'll get pulled by something anyway, I try to get pulled by things that build me instead of weaken me.

Example

Better to be addicted to sport, work, mastery or deep relationships than to social media, attention or numbing habits.

Decrease The Noise

Simplicity is removing what does not matter until only the essential remains.

How I use it

When life feels confusing, I look for noise - things to cut before I look for new solutions.

Example

Fewer tabs, fewer inputs, fewer random commitments, fewer people's opinions and fewer distractions between me and the thing I need to do. Last months been busy a lot doing everything. But now I'm cutting things out and focusing on the essential. Identified several points that were not essential and cut them out. Main ones are: late night going out, dating, smoking.

Devil Loves Empty Blocks

Empty time can easily turn into overthinking, avoidance or self-destruction if I leave it unmanaged.

How I use it

I try to give my days structure so I do not drift into useless thought loops.

Example

Planning my day in advance, filling open time with training, work, reading or going out instead of leaving whole chunks of the day undefined.

Not Everything You Want Will Be Good For You

Not everything I want is worth chasing.

How I use it

Not everything I want is worth chasing. Some things are obviously bad for me, like drinking, smoking, hooking up, or skipping the gym. But sometimes the more dangerous version is when I become deeply committed to something that looks impressive or meaningful without questioning whether it actually comes from me.

Example

This happened with my Polimi master's experience. I wanted it badly, and at one point it was almost not happening. Instead of stepping back and asking whether that might actually be a good thing, I kept pushing until I made it happen. In the end, I was not that glad I did.

Mood Follows Motion

I do not trust my mind to fix itself while sitting still.

How I use it

Movement changes state faster than thinking.

Example

If I feel mentally stuck, I walk, train, shower, or leave the room before trying to think my way out.

Environment Beats Willpower

My environment often decides my behavior before discipline even gets a chance.

How I use it

I design spaces that make good actions easier and bad actions harder.

Example

Phone away, clean desk, right seat, right music, right place.

Friction Reveals Truth

What I keep doing when it becomes inconvenient shows what I actually value.

How I use it

I do not judge myself by intentions. I judge by what survives friction.

Example

If I say I care about writing, training or business, I should still move toward it when tired, bored, or busy.

Action Clarifies

Clarity often comes after action, not before it.

How I use it

I start before I feel fully certain, because movement creates information.

Example

Sending the message, publishing the thing, going to the event or beginning the work before I feel fully ready.
